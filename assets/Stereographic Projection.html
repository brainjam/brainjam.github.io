There is a bijection between circles on the plane ℝ² and points in ℝ³ that are not inside the paraboloid z=x²+y². The circle with radius r and centre (x,y) maps to the point (x, y , x²+y²-r²). A line in ℝ³ maps to a pencil of co-axial circles. The circles intersect 1/2

or nest depending on whether the line goes through the paraboloid. Geometer Daniel Pedoe described this in a 1967 article “On A Theorem In Geometry”, pg 634. https://www.maa.org/programs/maa-awards/writing-awards/on-a-theorem-in-geometry

Pedoe, in his book Geometry discusses stereographic projection for the general quadric in section 87.1 and later on page 393 gives an explicit projective transform going from the paraboloid z=x2+y2z=x^2+y^2z=x2+y2 to the sphere x2+y2+z2=1x^2+y^2+z^2=1x2+y2+z2=1
